# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary

The purpose of this experiment was to evaluate whether the new onboarding campaign should be launched to all users. The performance of the Treatment group was compared with the Control group using different business metrics. The results showed that the Treatment group performed better in terms of paid conversions, engagement, and conversion speed.

---

## North Star Metric

The North Star Metric selected for this experiment is **Paid Conversion Rate**.

This metric was chosen because the main goal of the onboarding campaign is to increase the number of users who become paying customers. A higher conversion rate can help the company generate more revenue and grow its customer base.

---

## KPI Tree Explanation

The KPI tree was built around the Paid Conversion Rate.

The main drivers included:

### Acquisition Quality

* Landing Page Visit Rate
* Traffic Source Quality

### Activation Success

* Trial Start Rate
* Onboarding Completion Rate

### User Engagement and Experience

* Engagement Score
* Days to Convert

### Revenue Quality

* Average Revenue Per User
* Average Revenue Per Converted User

Guardrail metrics included Refund Rate, Support Ticket Rate, and Segment-Level Performance.

---

## Experiment Result Summary

The Treatment group performed better than the Control group on the main success metric.

* Control Paid Conversion Rate: 3.17%
* Treatment Paid Conversion Rate: 6.99%

The Treatment group also showed higher engagement and users converted in fewer days compared to the Control group.

---

## Hypothesis Test Interpretation

A hypothesis test was performed using Paid Conversion Rate.

The p-value obtained was **0.0006**, which is lower than the significance level of 0.05.

Since the p-value is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis was rejected. This means that the improvement seen in the Treatment group is statistically significant and is unlikely to have happened by chance.

---

## Guardrail Analysis

Along with conversion rate, other important metrics were also reviewed.

* Refund Rate increased slightly in the Treatment group.
* Support Ticket Rate was higher in the Treatment group.
* Engagement Score improved in the Treatment group.
* Days to Convert decreased, meaning users converted faster.

Although there are some risks, the overall performance of the Treatment group was positive.

---

## Segment-Level Insight

The results were also reviewed across different segments such as Region, Device Type, and Traffic Source.

No major segment showed a significant decline in performance. The Treatment group generally performed better across most segments.

---

## Final Recommendation

### Launch

Based on the analysis, I recommend launching the new onboarding campaign.

The Treatment group achieved a higher Paid Conversion Rate, better engagement, and faster conversion times. The hypothesis test also showed that the improvement is statistically significant.

The results suggest that the new onboarding campaign performs better than the current onboarding process.

---

## Risks and Limitations

* Support ticket rates were slightly higher in the Treatment group.
* Refund rates increased slightly.
* The analysis is based on the available experiment data and does not measure long-term customer behavior.
* Future monitoring will still be required after rollout.

---

## Next Steps

1. Launch the new onboarding campaign.
2. Monitor refund rates and support ticket rates after rollout.
3. Track customer engagement and retention over time.
4. Continue reviewing segment-level performance.
5. Run additional experiments to identify further improvements in the onboarding process.
